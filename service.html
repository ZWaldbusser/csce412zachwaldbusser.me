Service


My dedication to service didn't start in college, it started way earlier.
Throughout middle school and highschool, my parents would always, almost Faithfully, take our family to volunteer at the Central Texas Food Bank.
Be it packing orders, sorting dry goods, or checking fruit to see if it was still fresh, my family spent many Saturdays in that warehouse.
In addition to CTFB, I also worked closely with Operation Gratitude, whos mission is to "say Thank You to our Military and First Reponder communities".
Working with Operation Gratitude consisted of various projects, all aimed towards helping out our armed forces and veteran communities.

YMSL

I didn't join YMSL until my junior year of high school, as a "legacy" due to my younger brother having entered the organization.
As an older member (and new member), I had an incredibly steep hour requirement as oposed to my younger brother.
However despite this, I cleared the yearly service requirement with a margin of 15 hours.
Due to this fact (and my ability to help the Moms run Zoom meetings during COVID), I was selected out of all my peers to win the Heart of YMSL award, awarded once yearly.

MSC FISH

MSC FISH (Memorial Student Center Freshmen in Service and Hosting) is a FLO at Texas A&M
Dedicating itself to service, MSC FISH is the FLO that contributes the most to it's community.
In addition to having one of the highest hours requirements of any FLO, it also runs Kyle Field Day, which is an event that hosts tons of small-scall service projects and organizations.
I was in this organization for two years. Once as a member, and the following year as a staff member.
Through MSC FISH, I learned that despite being a busy college student, if you love something enough, you will make time for it. Be it service events or opportunities, I found ways to take part in both.
